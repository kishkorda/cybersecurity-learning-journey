This week, I dived into the world of malicious software and social engineering—two critical areas in understanding modern cyber threats.

I explored the different types of malicious software (malware), including viruses, worms, ransomware, and spyware, and learned how each one operates, spreads, and impacts systems. It was eye-opening to see how sophisticated and damaging these threats can be, especially when left undetected.

Alongside this, I studied social engineering—an often overlooked but highly effective method used by attackers. Understanding how manipulation and human psychology play a role in cyberattacks has completely changed the way I view online interactions. From phishing emails to impersonation tactics, it's clear that cybersecurity isn’t just about technology, but also about awareness and behavior.

This week reinforced an important lesson: staying secure is not just about having the right tools, but also about thinking critically and staying informed.
