

# Week 1: Understanding Cyber Security Principles

##  What is Cyber Security?
Cyber security is the practice of protecting systems, networks, devices, and data from cyber-attacks and unauthorised access. It involves using technologies, processes, and controls to reduce risks and prevent exploitation.

---

##  Brief History of Cyber Security
- **1970s:** First worm (Creeper) and first antivirus (Reaper)
- **1980s:** Development of cryptography (RSA algorithm)
- **1990s:** Rise of antivirus software and secure online transactions (SSL/HTTPS)
- **Present:** Increasing cyber threats and stricter regulations like GDPR

---

##  Key Cyber Security Concepts
- **Security:** Protecting systems and data from threats  
- **Identity:** Managing and verifying user access  
- **Threat:** Any malicious attempt to damage or steal data  
- **Vulnerability:** Weakness that can be exploited  
- **Risk:** Likelihood and impact of a threat  
- **Hazard:** Anything that could lead to a security breach  



##  CIA Triad (Core Principles)
- **Confidentiality:** Only authorised users can access data  
- **Integrity:** Data must be accurate and not altered  
- **Availability:** Systems and data must be accessible when needed  



##  Importance of Cyber Security
Cyber security is important because it protects:
- Personal data (PII)
- Financial information
- Health records
- Intellectual property
- Business and government systems  

Without it, organisations and individuals are vulnerable to cyber attacks.



##  Consequences of Poor Cyber Security

###  Economic Impact
- Financial loss from data breaches  
- Loss of business and revenue  

###  Reputational Damage
- Loss of customer trust  
- Damage to brand reputation  

###  Legal Consequences
- Fines and penalties (e.g., GDPR violations)  
- Legal action from affected individuals  



##  Key Takeaways
- Cyber security is essential in today’s digital world  
- Threats are constantly evolving  
- Strong security practices are required to protect systems and data  
- Organisations must take proactive steps to reduce risks
